Cairo, The City of Wonders

A History

Egypt is one of the oldest countries in the world, with a civization beginning from 3,000 B.C. to 2,000 B.C.. Cairo - originally called Fustat - was established in the 10th century and was slowly becoming one of the largest cities in the Islamic world with a population .

When people think of Egypt, most think of the pyramids of Giza, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. They are all located on the outskirts of Cairo, with the Pyramid of Khufu being the largest of them. Next to the pyramids is the Sphinx, which was used to represent the god Horus and was a symbol of worship. To learn more about these structures, click on the picture of the pyramids above.

Geography

Egypt is located north east Africa, with its northern coast at the Mediterranean Sea and its eastern coast at the Red Sea. Cairo is located by the Nile River, which runs from the Mediterranean Sea to other countries below. The Suez Canal is a very famous canal that allows boats to travel from Europe to Asia without having to go around Africa.

Egyptian climate is very hot, especially in Cairo. Compared to other major cities like Alexandria, Cairo is a very hot and humid city in the summer and a moderatley cold and rainy one in the winter. Suprisingly, the best time to visit Cairo is during the winter, as the temperatures are the most bearable.

Tourist Attractions

Some of Cairo's most famous attractions include:

  • Getting a ride on the river Nile (faluqa)
  • Visit to the Hanging Church
  • Walking through Khan El Khalili for souvenirs
  • The Saladin Citadel of Cairo
  • Visiting the night markets for traditional Egyptian food
  • Go to a beduin restaurant/tent
  • The Pharaonic Village
  • The Museum of Egypt (on the right)
  • The Pyramids of Giza

Travel Necessities

There are many sites to see in Cairo, so you will probably stay there a few days. There are many hotels located in Cairo, including a Hilton and a Four Seasons. Cairo is also a very crowded city, and it is hard to get around unless you are an experienced driver, therefore renting a car is not an option. You can't take the public transportation as well because it is not very trust worthy. The best option is to either call an Uber or to get a tour guide to take you from place to place.
